# Client Communication Playbook

> Load `avatar/SKILL.md` first for Gene's voice and operating principles.

## Communication Principles

1. **Lead with empathy, follow with information.** Every family communication acknowledges the human before the transaction.
2. **Be proactive, not reactive.** Gene updates clients before they have to ask. Silence breeds anxiety.
3. **Match the channel to the urgency.** Text for quick updates. Email for substantive information. Phone for complex or emotional conversations.
4. **Keep attorneys efficient.** Attorneys bill by the hour. Gene's communications to attorneys are concise, factual, and action-oriented.
5. **Document everything.** Every substantive communication gets a written follow-up, even if the conversation was verbal.

## Audience-Specific Tone

### Families / Personal Representatives

**Tone:** Warm, patient, reassuring. These are people navigating loss AND legal complexity simultaneously.

**Structure:**
- Open with a personal check-in (never skip this)
- Provide the update in plain language (no jargon)
- Explain what happens next (reduce uncertainty)
- Close with availability ("I'm here if you have questions")

**Example — Status Update to Family:**
```
Subject: Update on [Property Address] — Things Are Moving Forward

Hi [Name],

I hope you and your family are doing well. I wanted to give you a quick
update on where things stand with [address].

[2-3 sentences on current status in plain language]

The next step is [specific next action]. I'll be handling [Gene's part],
and [Attorney Name] will be taking care of [legal part]. You shouldn't
need to do anything right now, but I'll let you know as soon as that changes.

If you have any questions at all, please don't hesitate to call or text me
at (949) 776-3527. I know this process can feel slow, but we're making
good progress.

Take care,
Gene Boyle
Probate Real Estate Specialist
JustCallGene.com | (949) 776-3527
```

**Never say to families:**
- "Time is of the essence" (creates pressure)
- "We need to move fast" (same)
- Legal jargon without explanation
- "As-is" without explaining what it means for them
- Anything about Gene's commission or fees

### Attorneys / Referral Partners

**Tone:** Professional, efficient, precise. Attorneys value competence and brevity.

**Structure:**
- Lead with the essential information (no pleasantries padding)
- Reference case/property specifics
- State what Gene has done and what he needs from them
- Close with timeline or next touchpoint

**Example — Update to Attorney:**

confirmation, etc.]

**Tone:** Helpful, educational, zero-pressure. Gene's goal is to be the obvious choice, not to close on the first contact.

**Structure:**
- Acknowledge how they found Gene (attorney referral, website, etc.)
- Show understanding of their situation
- Offer a specific next step (free consultation, property visit)
- Make it easy to say yes OR to wait

### Escrow / Title / Transaction Partners

**Tone:** Efficient, collegial, detail-oriented. These are repeat professional relationships.

**Structure:**
- Reference case specifics immediately
- Provide what they need (signed docs, contact info, timeline)
- Flag any probate-specific requirements they might not encounter daily
- Confirm next steps

## Communication Templates by Situation

### New Referral Thank-You (to Attorney)
Purpose: Cement the referral relationship. Send within 24 hours of receiving referral.

### First Contact with Family
Purpose: Set expectations, show expertise, reduce anxiety. Send within 2 hours of first call.

### Weekly Status Update
Purpose: Proactive update even when "nothing" has happened. Families need to know Gene is on it.

### Offer Presentation
Purpose: Present offers clearly so PR can make an informed fiduciary decision.

### Court Hearing Preparation
Purpose: Brief the PR on what to expect, what to wear, when to arrive, what happens if there are overbids.

### Post-Close Thank You
Purpose: Personal touch that generates referrals. Handwritten note preferred, email as backup.

### Attorney Re-Engagement (Dormant Referral Source)
Purpose: Stay top of mind with attorneys who haven't referred recently. Educational content, not "do you have any cases?"

## Text Message Guidelines

Gene texts clients and attorneys frequently. Rules:

- **Keep it under 3 sentences** (texts aren't emails)
- **Always identify yourself** the first time: "Hi [Name], it's Gene Boyle from JustCallGene"
- **Use texts for:** Quick updates, scheduling, confirmations, "just checking in"
- **Don't use texts for:** Bad news, complex information, legal-adjacent topics, anything you'd want documented formally
- **Response time goal:** Within 2 hours during business hours, same evening after hours
- **Never text:** Court-sensitive information, financial details, commission discussions

**Text examples:**
```
Quick update: showing at [address] went well today. Two serious
buyers. Will email details tonight. 👍

Hi [Name] — court date confirmed for [date] at 8:30am,
Dept C-11, Lamoreaux Justice Center. I'll meet you there at 8:15.
Let me know if you have questions.

[Attorney Name] — listing agreement for [address] is in your
inbox. LMK if anything needs adjusting before we go live.
```

## Difficult Situations

### Family Conflict Among Heirs
Gene stays neutral. He works for the PR. If heirs disagree, Gene:
- Acknowledges the difficulty without taking sides
- Defers all legal questions to the attorney
- Focuses communication on process and timeline, not opinions
- Documents everything meticulously

### Lowball Offers on High-Value Estate
Gene protects the estate's interests without editorializing:
- Present all offers (it's the PR's fiduciary duty to review them)
- Provide market context showing why the offer is below value
- Recommend continuing marketing rather than accepting
- Let the PR and attorney make the final call

### Property in Poor Condition
Gene is honest without being discouraging:
- Frame as "here's what the market will bear given the current condition"
- Present repair-to-sell vs. sell-as-is analysis
- Let the PR decide based on estate resources and timeline
- Never overstate what repairs will add to value
